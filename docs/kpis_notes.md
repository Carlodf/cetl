# Sales metrics

Measure performance of sale activities of retail business.

- ADS (Average Dollar Sales): used to calculate the average amount of money spent by a customer in a single transaction.
  - ADS = NetSales ($) / Number of Transactions
  - GrossSales = price per unit \* unit sold
  - NetSales = GrossSales - (Discounts + Sales return + Allowances)
- APW (Average Per Week): indicator of how much inventory you need to keep on hand to meet demand and avoid stock-outs.
  - Gross Sale units / Number of Weeks
- WOS (Weeks of Supply): indicator of how long the inventory will last, when high might indicate the business has too much inventory, when los indicate that might run out of stock.
  - WOS = Current Inventory / Average daily Sales (units)
- Maintained Markup: used to evaluate the profitability of a produc and make informed decisions about how to price them.
  - Maintained Markup = Gross Profit / Retail Price
